Gwyneth Paltrow puts Montecito guesthouse on Airbnb

Publish date: 2024-07-07

Explore More

Now you can really live the Goop life.

In a paid partnership Instagram post on Tuesday, Gwyneth Paltrow announced that she was about to list the “beautiful little guest house” to her sprawling Montecito, California, home on Airbnb.

Paltrow spends the video post walking viewers through the cozy abode — which includes a soaking tub, wood-burning fireplace, vaulted ceilings and Goop skincare products.

She then shows off a wine room and says that Airbnb guests could join her and her husband, Brad Falchuk, there for dinner.

“Loneliness is a human condition, but in the past few years, increased isolation and our lack of community has made our lives even more fragmented. @airbnb had the brilliant idea of doing something to make the world a little less lonely, which is why I’m inviting you to come stay at my Montecito guesthouse for a night,” Paltrow states in the caption of the post.

“While we may begin as strangers, I hope we’ll find connections and commonalities over a delicious meal. Lay by the pool, go on one of my favorite hikes and of course you’ll have a bathroom stocked with my favorite @goop products for a truly luxuriating stay,” Paltrow continued. “Hope to host you soon.”

On the Airbnb listing for the “Goop-inspired Stay with Gwyneth Paltrow,” various activities — guided meditation, spa day, a Goop-inspired meal — are listed as a way of helping guests achieve “the ultimate Goop retreat.”

Paltrow also promises to be attentive to any urgent requirements guests may have, such as accommodating allergies or dietary restrictions.

The booking for the one-night stay for two guests at Paltrow’s Montecito guesthouse opens 10 a.m. PT on Tuesday, Aug. 15. The stay will take place on Saturday, Sept. 9.

Paltrow and Falchuk purchased the Montecito property as a teardown for $4.9 million for 2016.

They then spent several years building a 14,000-square-foot “eco mansion” on the grounds.

It includes the guest house and an Olympic-sized swimming pool.
